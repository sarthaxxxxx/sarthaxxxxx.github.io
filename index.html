About

Hey! I'm Sarthak Kumar Maharana, an incoming Master of Science student in Electrical Engineering with a focus on Signal and Image Processing at the Ming Hsieh Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering, University of Southern California.

My research interests lie in signal processing, with more inclination towards speech processing, and in image processing, where my major focus is on medical images. However, my target at grad school is to explore one of these areas and to propel my career in the same direction. I intend to pursue a PhD later on or enter as an industry researcher at a company that values research and development.

Before USC, I obtained my Bachelor's in Electrical and Electronics Engineering from IIIT Bhubaneswar, India. For my Bachelor's thesis, where I was at SPIRE Lab, Indian Institute of Science, Bangalore, I worked on developing acoustic-to-articulatory (AAI) models for dysarthric speech and got the wonderful opportunity to publish as a first-author and virtually present my work at the prestigious IEEE ICASSP 2021.

Currently, I'm working as a part-time Research Assistant at the Medical Mechatronics Lab, Department of Biomedical Engineering, National University of Singapore, where my research revolves around graph representation learning applications to rat OCT images.

To find out about my academic/professional life, please grab a copy of my resume and to get a list of my recent work, check out my GitHub.
